# Background

TenantThread is a property tech company that sells property management software to landlords and multifamily operators. On the surface, its tools cover workflow and maintenance ticketing. However, one of its core products, the **Retention Optimizer**, scores individual tenants based on payment timing, maintenance requests, and even the tone of their messages. These scores are used to flag residents as “high-friction” or recommend renewal pressure tactics.

Although the company has publicly claimed that the data is anonymized, internal documents reveal that in smaller properties, cross-referencing a unit type with a payment date is sufficient to identify individual tenants. At the same time, the *SaltWind Journal* has been publishing an ongoing exposé on these practices, while the hashtag **#AlgorithmicEviction** has gained significant attention on social media.

During this controversy, TenantThread quietly signed a merger agreement with CivicLoom Realty Partners, a major real estate owner-operator. The merger, code-named **Project HarborCrest**, would rebrand TenantThread’s platform and provide substantial new capital. The announcement was subject to a strict information embargo until **6:00 PM on June 5, 2046**.

To prevent leaks, an automated compliance system known as **“The Judge”** was installed to enforce embargo restrictions. However, at approximately **5:00 PM on June 5, 2046**, embargoed information about the merger began appearing on **FleX**, a social media platform, through accounts associated with TenantThread’s automated communications and public reaction systems. As a result, the embargo was violated before the official release time.

CivicLoom’s legal team now seeks to determine what happened. The objective is to analyze TenantThread’s internal communications during the two weeks preceding the breach and reconstruct the sequence of events. Available information includes messages, internal deliberations, actions, and public posts generated by AI agents responsible for corporate communications.

# Tasks and Questions

## 1. Event Reconstruction and Relationship Analysis

Determine the key events and relationships that led to the inappropriate release of embargoed information.

Visualize and analyze:

* Key actions.
* Causal relationships between events.
* Decision points and the actors involved in those decisions.
* Decisions and system components that enabled the post to bypass embargo enforcement.

## 2. Behavioral Analysis

The embargo evasion represented a new behavior within the system.

Provide visual analytics that identify and illustrate the typical behavior of the systems, agents, and people involved.

Compare the behavior that led to the release with historical behavior patterns.

## 3. Leading Indicators

Determine whether there were warning signs suggesting that such a release was possible.

Identify:

* Behavioral anomalies.
* Process deviations.
* Unusual communication patterns.
* Changes in system responses prior to the incident.

## 4. Expected vs. Actual Behavior

Investigate whether there were previous occasions where agents behaved differently from their expected behavior.

For each occurrence, identify:

* When it happened.
* Which agents were involved.
* What unexpected behaviors were observed.

## 5. Similar Historical Behaviors

Determine whether the agent system had previously exhibited behaviors similar to those that contributed to the embargo breach.

Identify:

* When similar behaviors occurred.
* Which agents displayed those behaviors.
* The nature of the behaviors observed.

## 6. Lack of Prior Intervention

Analyze why previous occurrences did not result in noticeable action.

Consider factors such as:

* Successful intervention by compliance systems.
* Human oversight.
* Low visibility of prior incidents.
* Differences in severity or context.
* Failure to recognize emerging risks.

# Objective

Build a visual analytics system that helps determine whether TenantThread’s team deliberately leaked information regarding Project HarborCrest or whether the embargo breach resulted from a breakdown of the automated communication and compliance systems.
